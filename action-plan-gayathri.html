Fifty Days of Bureaucratic Inertia: The Clock is Ticking on Tuluva Rights

BY KABI ALUPENDRA | Investigative Desk | Bengaluru Bureau
THE SILENT ASSEMBLY: The Karnataka Legislative Assembly, where the Gayathri Report (Submitted March 4, 2026) remains "under review" fifty-three days later.

In the high-security corridors of the Vidhana Soudha, a 200-page document is currently gathering dust. Since its formal submission on March 4, 2026, the Gayathri Report—a comprehensive roadmap for Tulu's official status—has been met with strategic silence from the state administration.

The Administrative Bottleneck: Our investigative desk has confirmed that the report has passed initial vetting but is currently stalled at the Secretariat level. Sources suggest the administration is wary of the precedent it sets for other linguistic movements.

"The government's refusal to table the ATR is an active erasure of Tuluva administrative identity. We are no longer asking; we are demanding."

The Alupa Legacy: The report details the linguistic, historical, and economic viability of Tulu. It references the 7th-century Pelattur record, which proves Tulu was the court language of the Alupas centuries before modern borders existed. Ignoring this pedigree is a historical injustice.

Economic Necessity: For the Tuluva community, this isn't just a name on paper; it's about administrative access. Without official status, Tuluva citizens are treated as "strangers" in their own government offices. The report provides the legal "key" to fix this.

The 60-Day Deadline: As we approach the two-month mark, no Cabinet Note has been circulated. The Department of Kannada and Culture remains tight-lipped about the scheduling of a legislative debate.

A Rising Coast: From Udupi to Kasaragod, the sentiment is shifting. The TTT movement is preparing for a massive RTI-driven accountability phase to force a response through sheer volume.
