Fifty Days of Bureaucratic Inertia: The Clock is Ticking on Tuluva Rights

BY KABI ALUPENDRA | Investigative Desk
THE SILENT ASSEMBLY: The Karnataka Legislative Assembly, where the Gayathri Report (Submitted March 4, 2026) remains "under review" fifty-three days later. (Photo: TTT Archive)

In the high-security corridors of the Vidhana Soudha, a 200-page document is currently gathering dust. Since its formal submission on March 4, 2026, the Gayathri Report—a comprehensive roadmap for Tulu's official status—has been met with strategic silence. This "60-Day Silence" is now being viewed by legal analysts not as a technical delay, but as a political evasion.

The report details the linguistic, historical, and economic viability of Tulu. According to administrative norms, once a committee submits a report, the state must present an Action Taken Report (ATR) in the Assembly. By remaining silent, the administration is bypassing its duty to the people of Tulunad.
