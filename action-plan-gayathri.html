Investigation: Part I

Fifty Days of Bureaucratic Inertia: The Clock is Ticking on Tuluva Rights

BY KABI ALUPENDRA | Investigative Desk | Bengaluru Bureau
THE SILENT ASSEMBLY: The Karnataka Legislative Assembly, where the Gayathri Report remains "under review" fifty-three days after its formal handover to the State Government.

In the high-security corridors of the Vidhana Soudha, a 200-page document is currently gathering dust. Since its formal submission on March 4, 2026, the Gayathri Report—a scientific and historical masterpiece designed to secure Tulu’s official status—has been met with strategic silence.

This "60-Day Silence" is now being characterized by legal analysts and TTT activists not merely as a technical delay, but as a deliberate act of political evasion. Under standard parliamentary ethics, a report of this magnitude should have seen a Cabinet Note issued within the first three weeks.

"The government's refusal to acknowledge the March 4 submission is an active erasure of the Tuluva administrative identity."

The Administrative Bottleneck: Our investigative desk has confirmed that the report has passed the initial vetting stage but is currently stalled at the Secretariat level. Sources suggest that the administration is wary of the precedent it sets for other linguistic movements, choosing to ignore the unique "Classical" pedigree and historical court-language status that Tulu holds.

Historical Lineage: The report draws heavily from the Pelattur stone inscription, a 7th-century artifact that proves Tulu was the court language of the early Alupa kings. To ignore the Gayathri Report is to ignore 1,400 years of coastal administrative history. The transition of Tulu-Tigalari into Unicode 16.0 further cements the digital necessity of this recognition.

Economic and Legal Necessity: Without official status under Article 345, Tuluva students are deprived of state-funded higher education resources, and coastal residents remain "strangers" in their own government offices. The Gayathri Report provides the legal "key" to fix this, yet the door remains locked from the inside by bureaucrats who fear coastal sovereignty.

The TTT Strategy: From Udupi to Kasaragod, the sentiment is shifting. The TTT movement has already begun drafting legal notices to serve to the State Secretariat. If the Action Taken Report (ATR) is not presented by the next session, activists plan to flood the Secretariat with thousands of individual RTI inquiries, forcing a response through sheer volume of law.
