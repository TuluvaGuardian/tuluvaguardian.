Fifty Days of Bureaucratic Inertia: The Clock is Ticking on Tuluva Rights

BY KABI ALUPENDRA | Investigative Desk | Bengaluru Bureau
THE SILENT ASSEMBLY: The Karnataka Legislative Assembly, where the Gayathri Report remains "under review" fifty-three days after submission.

In the high-security corridors of the Vidhana Soudha, a 200-page document is currently gathering dust. Since its formal submission on March 4, 2026, the Gayathri Report has been met with strategic silence. Legal analysts suggest this is no longer a technical delay, but a political evasion.

According to administrative norms, once a report is submitted, the state must present an Action Taken Report (ATR). By remaining silent, the administration is bypassing its duty to the people of Tulunad.
