The ATR: The Final Barrier to Tulu Sovereignty

Most citizens believe that submitting a report is the final step. It is not. Under the Commissions of Inquiry Act and standard parliamentary procedure, a report is "dead air" until the government produces an Action Taken Report (ATR).

What is an ATR?

The Action Taken Report is a mandatory document that the Ministry must present to the Legislative Assembly. It lists every recommendation in the Gayathri Report and states clearly:
✅ Accepted: We will implement this.
❌ Rejected: We refuse this (and we must explain why).

The Violation: By not drafting an ATR for the Gayathri Report (Submitted Mar 4), the Cabinet is preventing our MLAs from voting on Tulu's official status.

How to Trigger a "Digital Mandamus"

If the government fails in its Public Duty (the ATR), the High Court of Karnataka can intervene. You don't need to travel to Bengaluru. You can alert the judiciary via the Online Grievance Portal.

STEP 1: Document the Default

In your complaint, cite that the Gayathri Report (Ref: Mar 4, 2026) has exceeded the customary 21-day window for a Cabinet Note. This is a "Failure of Administrative Duty."

STEP 2: Use the "Suo Motu" Request

Ask the Court to take Suo Motu (voluntary) notice of this delay. State that it affects the cultural rights of 1.8 million Tulu speakers under Article 29 and Article 345.
