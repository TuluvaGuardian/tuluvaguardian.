Special Investigation: Language Rights

The Gayathri Report: A Legislative Blueprint for Tulu Official Status

The Karnataka Assembly in Bengaluru, where the resolution for Tulu's official status is expected to be debated.

BENGALURU/UDUPI: The submission of the K.M. Gayathri Committee report on March 4, 2026, has provided the first definitive legal roadmap for Tulu's inclusion as an additional official language. Using the "Andhra Model," the report outlines how local MLAs can move a resolution under Article 345.

Key Mechanism: An MLA must propose an amendment to the Karnataka Official Languages Act, 1963. The Gayathri report provides the necessary administrative proof that Tulu recognition will not disrupt existing Kannada-medium systems.

Field studies conducted in early 2026 confirmed that declaring Tulu as a second additional official language in Udupi and Dakshina Kannada would allow for government circulars, bus destination boards, and official announcements to be printed in Tulu-Tigalari script, ensuring the script's survival for future generations.
