The submission of the K.M. Gayathri Committee report on March 4, 2026, has fundamentally shifted the battle for Tulu's recognition from a cultural movement to a technical administrative process. This 200-page document serves as the "Impact Study" that local MLAs have lacked for decades.

The Andhra Secretariat Model

In January 2026, the commission conducted a field study in the Andhra Pradesh Secretariat to study the integration of Urdu. Their findings confirm that a "Second Additional Official Language" status is the most efficient path for Tulu. This model ensures that administrative costs are kept low while maximizing the visibility of the Tulu-Tigalari script in public spaces like government offices and bus stations.

Legislative Timeline: The Article 345 Path

Constitutional experts interviewed by the Tuluva Guardian suggest the resolution will follow a specific mechanical path:

  • Introduction: A private member's bill to amend the Karnataka Official Languages Act, 1963.
  • The Gayathri Evidence: Using the March 2026 report to prove that Tulu recognition will not conflict with the primary status of Kannada.
  • Implementation: Mandating Tulu-Tigalari signage and official notifications in Udupi and Dakshina Kannada districts.

By moving this resolution, coastal legislators are not just fighting for a language; they are reviving the administrative heritage of the Alupa dynasty for the 21st century.
