Legislative Special Report

The Gayathri Report: A Technical Victory for Tulu Recognition

DECISIVE DATA: The 200-page K.M. Gayathri Committee report acts as the "Impact Study" required for an Article 345 resolution.

BENGALURU: The March 4, 2026 submission of the Gayathri report has fundamentally changed the conversation regarding the official status of Tulu. For the first time, local legislators are armed with technical data rather than just cultural sentiment.

The committee's research in the Andhra Pradesh Secretariat has proven that "Additional Official Language" status is administratively feasible without disrupting current state systems. This "Andhra Model" provides a Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) for utilizing Tulu in local government signage and circulars.

The Article 345 Strategy

The report confirms that an MLA-led resolution under Article 345 can legally designate Tulu for official use in Udupi and Dakshina Kannada. This shift ensures the survival of the Tulu-Tigalari script, linking modern administration back to the historical Alupa dynasty heritage.
