The Gayathri Report: A Legislative Blueprint for Tulu

By Tuluva Guardian Research Unit | Updated: April 22, 2026

UDUPI/BENGALURU: The submission of the K.M. Gayathri Committee report on March 4, 2026, has fundamentally changed the conversation regarding the official status of Tulu. This high-level committee has formally endorsed the state's move to grant Tulu the status of a Second Additional Official Language.

The "Andhra Model" Explained

The core of the Gayathri report is based on a field study conducted in the Andhra Pradesh Secretariat in January 2026. The committee analyzed how Andhra Pradesh successfully integrated Urdu as an additional language without conflict. This model provides three key advantages for Tulu:

  • No Financial Burden: Proves that recognition doesn't require massive immediate spending.
  • Administrative Clarity: Provides the "Standard Operating Procedure" (SOP) for using Tulu in government letters, boards, and public announcements in Udupi and Dakshina Kannada.
  • Legal Precedent: Uses existing Indian administrative frameworks that have already survived High Court scrutiny.

How the Resolution Moves (Article 345)

Local MLAs now have the expert data required to move an official resolution in the Karnataka Legislative Assembly. Under Article 345 of the Indian Constitution, the process is as follows:

  1. Introduction of Amendment: An MLA proposes an amendment to the Karnataka Official Languages Act, 1963.
  2. The Gayathri Defense: The MLA cites the March 2026 report to prove that Tulu is not just a dialect, but a language with a 3,000-year history and its own unique script.
  3. The Vote: With Speaker U.T. Khader and 13 coastal legislators already in favor, the resolution seeks a simple majority to make Tulu legally "Official" in the coast.

"We are no longer asking for a favor," says a Tuluva Guardian analyst. "The Gayathri Commission has provided the evidence; the Article 345 resolution is now the mechanical final step."
