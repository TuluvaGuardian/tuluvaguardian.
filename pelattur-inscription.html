Epigraphy & Linguistic Evolution

Deciphering Pelattur: From 'Tatthu' to 'Nēmakathi'

THE PELATTUR RECORD: This 7th-century stone is more than a legal decree; it is a timestamp for the Tulu language. By analyzing the transition of administrative verbs, we can bridge the gap between Alupa governance and modern Tuluva identity.

"...Arasu moodillaya kilatura iruvatta naluveren tatthu ijistanadeka..."

The Evolution of Authority

In the Pelattur inscription, the verb 'Tatthu' is used to describe the selection or positioning of the 24 council members. In modern Tulu, this administrative function has evolved into a more formalized term.

7th Century Archaic
Tatthu
(Selected / Placed)
➞
21st Century Modern
Nēmakathi (ನೇಮಕಾತಿ)
(Appointment / Recruitment)

The transition from Tatthu to Nēmakathi represents the professionalization of Tulu administration. While the root changed, the Council of 24 remains the historical ancestor of our modern representative boards.

Comparative Administrative Glossary

7th Century Term Modern Tulu Equivalent Function
Tatthu Nēmakathi (ನೇಮಕಾತಿ) The act of official appointment.
Arasu Rajae / Arasu Sovereign head of state.

Why This Matters for Article 345

Opponents of Tulu's official recognition often claim it lacks an "administrative register." The Pelattur inscription shatters this. By showing a clear line from Tatthu to Nēmakathi, we prove that Tulu has possessed the vocabulary for governance, law, and recruitment for over fourteen centuries.

"If the Alupas could conduct a 'Nēmakathi' (recruitment) for a Council of 24 in the 700s using Tulu, the state can certainly conduct its administration in Tulu in 2026."
THE VERDICT: Tulu is not just a language of the past; it is a language of continuous authority.
