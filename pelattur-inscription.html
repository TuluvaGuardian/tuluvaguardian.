Arasu moodillaya kilatura iruvatta naluveren tatthu ijistanadekaaperunarasuku samudaya iruv adikarigu astanatutu eraboduvendhu arasu adhikari upuppanata varandeva perbappena bāmana pervarasanu kerustu bramher utti varanasitdu sara kabilen kerustu pasunu ade kuntattanayagu." </div> <section> <h2>The Administrative Autopsy: 1,400 Years of Governance</h2> <p>The mention of the <strong>"Iruvatta Naluveren"</strong> (The Twenty-Four) is perhaps the most significant political revelation in the Pelattur archive. This was a structured <strong>Administrative Council</strong> from the Kilattur block. In an era of absolute autocracy, the Alupa state practiced a form of decentralized governance that pre-dates modern parliamentary systems.</p> <section style="background: #fffaf0; border-left: 5px solid #800000; padding: 25px; margin: 30px 0;"> <h2 style="margin-top: 0; border: none;">The Story: The Great Trial of Pelattur</h2> <p>The inscription records a high-stakes legal drama. A nobleman with the massive compound title <strong>Varandeva Perbappena Bamanna Pervarasa</strong> stood accused of a capital crime. To ensure justice, the officer <strong>Uruppana</strong> convened the Parliament of 24. The trial concluded with a guilty verdict, sentencing the official and invoking an eternal curse: anyone who interfered with this decree would bear the sin of slaying a thousand cows in Varanasi. This proves Tulu was the <strong>Language of Command</strong>.</p> </section> <div style="display: grid; grid-template-columns: 1fr 1fr; gap: 20px; margin: 20px 0;"> <div style="background: #f9f9f9; padding: 15px; border-top: 3px solid #1a1a1a;"> <strong>Check & Balance System</strong><br> For a king to convene 24 representatives for a judicial sentencing proves that law was a collective responsibility, not a royal whim. </div> <div style="background: #f9f9f9; padding: 15px; border-top: 3px solid #1a1a1a;"> <strong>Judicial Sovereignty</strong><br> The inscription details the punishment of a high-ranking individual for a capital crime, confirming Tulu as the <strong>Language of Law</strong>. </div> </div> </section> <section style="background: #f4f4f4; padding: 25px; border: 1px solid #ddd; margin: 30px 0;"> <h2 style="margin-top: 0;">Archaic Tulu: The Linguistic Frontier</h2> <table> <thead> <tr> <th>Archaic Form (7th c.)</th> <th>Modern Interpretation</th> <th>Significance</th> </tr> </thead> <tbody> <tr> <td>Arasu</td> <td>Arasu / Rajae</td> <td>Standardized title for Sovereignty.</td> </tr> <tr> <td>Adikari</td> <td>Adhikari</td> <td>Continuity of administrative roles.</td> </tr> <tr> <td>Tatthu</td> <td>Nēmaka (Appointment)</td> <td>Archaic verb for positioning/selection.</td> <tr> <td style="font-weight:bold;">Tatthu</td> <td><strong>Nēmakathi (ನೇಮಕಾತಿ)</strong></td> <td>The archaic root for selection has evolved into the modern term for <strong>Recruitment/Appointment</strong>, showing 1,400 years of administrative continuity.</td> </tr> </tr> </tbody> </table> <p style="font-style: italic; color: #555;">Note: The evolution from <strong>Tatthu</strong> to the modern <strong>Nēmaka</strong> shows the natural aging of a living language.</p> <section> <h2>The Tuluva Parliament: A Legacy of Democracy</h2> <p>The mention of the <strong>"Iruvatta Naluveren"</strong> (The Twenty-Four) is the most significant political revelation in the Pelattur archive. This was not a mere gathering of elders; it was a <strong>Tuluva Parliament</strong>. While much of the world was under absolute autocracy, the Alupa state practiced a decentralized model where a council represented the local interests of the Kilattur block.</p> <div style="display: grid; grid-template-columns: 1fr 1fr; gap: 20px; margin: 20px 0;"> <div style="background: #f9f9f9; padding: 15px; border-top: 3px solid #800000;"> <strong>Check & Balance System</strong><br> The Council acted as a check on royal power.
