The Shudra Stigma: How Caste Myths, Elite Neglect, and Buried History Silenced Tulu

By Tuluva Guardian Investigations | May 2026
Prologue: When a Civilization Is Told Its Language Is “Low”
Not every language is conquered by armies. Some are conquered by ideas.

That is the story of Tulu. For nearly two centuries, Tulu was made to wear a social label: “Shudra Bhasha.”

I. The Manufacture of a Stigma

Following the fall of the Keladi Nayakas, a new socio-political elite emerged. Seeking prestige in colonial structures, they rebranded Tulu as a "Shudra tongue"—a language fit only for the fields, never the courtroom.

II. The Stone That Shattered the Myth

The Pelatur Inscription proved that in the 7th century, under the Alupas, Tulu was carved in royal stone. It was a language of statecraft long before the post-Keladi elites sought to silence it.

III. The First Rebel: S.U. Paniyadi

In 1928, S.U. Paniyadi organized the Tulu Mahasabha to challenge the assumption that Tulu lacked textual discipline. He proved the language had architecture through the Tulu Vyakarana.

IV. The Intellectual Revolution

Dr. Palthady Puncithaya identified the "Six Pillars" of Tulu classical literature, proving a literacy chain from the 11th century. Meanwhile, Ravindra Mundkur traced Tuluva roots back to ancient global trade routes.
