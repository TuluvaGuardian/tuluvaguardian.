The Shudra Stigma: How Caste Myths, Elite Neglect, and Buried History Silenced Tulu

By Tuluva Guardian Investigations | May 2026
Prologue: When a Civilization Is Told Its Language Is “Low”
Not every language is conquered by armies. Some are conquered by ideas.

That is the story of Tulu. For nearly two centuries, Tulu was made to wear a social label: “Shudra Bhasha.”

I. The Manufacture of a Stigma: The Post-Keladi Erasure

Following the fall of the Keladi Nayakas, a new socio-political elite emerged. Seeking prestige in colonial structures, they rebranded Tulu as a "Shudra tongue"—a language fit only for the fields, never the courtroom or the academy.

II. The Stone That Shattered the Myth

The Pelatur Inscription proves that in the 7th century, under the Alupas, Tulu was carved in royal stone. It was a language of statecraft long before the post-Keladi elites sought to silence it.

III. The First Rebel: S.U. Paniyadi

In 1928, S.U. Paniyadi did the unthinkable: he declared that Tulu did not need a "parent" language. By organizing the Tulu Mahasabha, he shifted the conversation from culture to politics.

The Paniyadi Doctrine:
  • Tulu Vyakarana: Proving the language's independent internal logic.
  • Script Advocacy: Demanding the reclamation of Tulu-Tigalari.
  • The Rebellion: Fighting the "deliberate silencing" by the state.

IV. The Intellectual Revolution

The Guardians of Civilizational Data

Dr. Palthady Puncithaya identified the chronological "Six Pillars" of Tulu classical literature, proving a literacy chain from the 11th to 17th centuries.

The Tulu Classical Canon
  1. Devi Mahatme – By the Adi Kavi of Tulu.
  2. Tulu Mahabharato – By Arunabja (14th C).
  3. Karnaparvo – By King Harihara (Royal Patronage).
  4. Sri Bhagavatho – By Vishnutunga (17th C).
  5. Kaveri – Classical poetic architecture.
  6. Tulu Ramayana – Completing the Classical Triad.

Ravindra Mundkur provided the Scientific Defense. As a Digital Pioneer, he used maritime history and toponymy to trace Tuluva roots back to the ancient Mediterranean trade routes.

P.S. Rai built the lexical fortress with dictionaries that proved Tulu’s core vocabulary was entirely original.
